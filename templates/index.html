Understanding the Dictionary

Learn more about the specific conditions we check for and the self-care techniques we recommend.

Anxiety Disorder
Characterized by excessive, persistent worry and fear about everyday situations. It can cause physical symptoms like a rapid heart rate and interfere with daily living and focus.
Bipolar Disorder
A complex disorder associated with extreme mood swings that include emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and severe lows (depression), affecting energy levels and sleep.
Depression
A mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness, emptiness, and loss of interest in activities once enjoyed. It heavily impacts how you feel, think, and handle daily activities.
PTSD
Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) is triggered by experiencing or witnessing a terrifying event. Symptoms may include flashbacks, severe nightmares, and uncontrollable thoughts.
Schizophrenia
A serious mental disorder affecting a person's ability to think, feel, and behave clearly. It may result in some combination of hallucinations, delusions, and extremely disordered thinking.
4-4-4-4 Box Breathing
A powerful stress-relief technique to calm your nervous system. How to do it: Inhale deeply for 4 seconds, hold your breath for 4 seconds, exhale slowly for 4 seconds, and hold empty for 4 seconds. Repeat.
5-4-3-2-1 Grounding
A mindfulness method designed to calm panic and anxiety. How to do it: Identify 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, and 1 you can taste to bring yourself back to the present.
